Overview, Why & Goals

The market is crowded — but broken in the same ways everywhere. SportsEngine, TeamSnap, and LeagueApps collectively hold ~45% of the youth sports software market, yet their review pages read like a shared complaint department: coaches who hate the interface, admins who spend hours on tasks that should take minutes, parents who receive ads inside a product they paid for, and support teams that respond to urgent game-day issues with a bot. The incumbents have optimized for feature count and investor metrics — not for the volunteer running a 10-team baseball org on a Tuesday night.

PrizumSports starts from a different premise: the user is a volunteer, not a software operator. Every design decision flows from that.

Why PrizumSports — competitive differentiation
🎯 Volunteer-first design Core moat

Every platform pitches features. Nobody pitches simplicity as a core value. Admins report spending hours on TeamSnap trying to complete tasks that should take minutes. SportsEngine's own coaches "hate, hate, hate" uploading schedules.

PrizumSports: every common task completable in <3 min, no documentation required.
⚾ GameChanger-native architecture Unique positioning

GameChanger serves over 1M teams across 7M contests. Every competitor treats it as a rival. PrizumSports treats it as infrastructure — coaches stay in the app they already love, and iCal aggregation creates the org-level master calendar no off-the-shelf tool handles.

The only platform that says "keep using GameChanger" instead of "replace it."
🏢 Multi-org backbone Market gap

Every competitor sells to individual clubs. A county-level baseball association with 12 member clubs is an entirely different and underserved buyer. PrizumSports is designed so Prizum serves as the shared member and payment backbone — orgs share infrastructure without sharing data.

Add new orgs without code changes. Prizum provisions, iCal connects, dashboard activates.
💰 Transparent pricing — no penalty model Trust differentiator

SportsEngine charges customers more for not using certain features. TeamSnap shows ads to paying users. As both platforms focus on monetizing their base more aggressively, the opening for a clean, flat-fee alternative is real and widening.

No ads. No penalty charges. No upsell pressure. One price covers everything.
🧑‍💼 Real human support at volunteer hours Retention driver

Competitor support desks are closed when volunteer admins have their crises — weeknights and weekends. "One phone call would resolve this, but instead they email back and forth for 2 days" is a real review of both SportsEngine and TeamSnap.

Human support available evenings and weekends — when the org actually needs it.
🏟️ Umpire scheduling — future wedge Phase 4 moat

No major platform handles umpire scheduling in an integrated way — it's spreadsheets, separate apps, or phone calls. Once umpires are in the system, a network effect emerges: umpires available to multiple orgs on the same platform creates a moat that compounds over time.

Phase 4 capability — no competitor does this integrated with facility + game calendar.
🤖 AI that gives volunteers their Tuesday nights back Phase 2–3 differentiator
AI Communication Intelligence

Volunteer admins write the same emails every season. AI drafts them. "Generate a season kickoff email for 12U families" pulls from org info, team roster, and upcoming schedule — producing a ready-to-send message in seconds. Natural language segmentation replaces filter-maze UX: "send this to families with an unpaid waiver and a payment plan installment due this week" works as a plain English command.

Drafts comms · natural language segments · anomaly alerts (e.g. "registration is 23% behind last year's pace")
AI Parent Self-Service on Org Website

Parents ask the same five questions constantly — and every one of those questions currently lands in a volunteer admin's inbox. An AI layer on the org website answers them instantly: schedule lookups, payment status, registration deadlines, field locations, and waiver completion. This is the one place a chat interface genuinely earns its keep — deflecting volunteer time, not replacing customer support.

"When does 10U practice?" · "How do I update my payment plan?" · Routes to Prizum workflows automatically

Risks & Mitigations

HIGH

GameChanger CSV or iCal access changes

GC has no obligation to maintain CSV formats or iCal availability. If either changes, roster sync and calendar aggregation break.

→ Maintain Prizum as master record always. Run automated weekly exports. GC is downstream-only.

HIGH

Volunteer adoption failure

Introducing too many new tools simultaneously causes reversion to spreadsheets and group texts.

→ Phase 1 introduces two new things max. Coaches only use GC. Record Loom walkthroughs, not manuals.

MED

Admin dashboard scope creep

Teams try to rebuild SaaS features inside the custom dashboard, creating a bloated, fragile codebase.

→ Dashboard scope = aggregate + link, not rebuild. If Prizum has a good UI, link to it.

MED

Prizum Sports vendor dependency

Consolidating registrations, members, facilities, and comms into one vendor means price or API changes affect three functional areas at once.

→ Maintain full export capability. Confirm API access in contract. The consolidation also reduces integration risk significantly.

MED

Data drift between Prizum and GC mid-season

Roster changes in Prizum don't auto-update GC. Admins can end up with two versions of the roster truth.

→ Prizum owns player data; GC is downstream copy. Document roster-change process. Run sync audits mid-season.

LOW

iCal feed management at scale

A growing list of manually-added GC iCal URLs can become burdensome. Stale feeds cause teams to silently disappear from the master calendar.

→ Build iCal management as a first-class feature with status indicators, last-sync timestamps, and staleness alerts.

LOW

Parent communication fragmentation

Parents receiving messages from multiple channels erodes trust in any single channel.

→ Day-one policy: GC for game-day chat, Prizum for org-wide comms. Communicate the channel map at season start.
